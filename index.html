Semantic Elements Summary

Semantic elements are used to clearly indicate the role of the content on the page. Semantic HTML tags such as footer, header, and nav distinctly tell the developer and the browser what the meaning of the element is.

Non-semantic elements, such as div and span, do not describe what role they play on the page. Non-semantic elements can be displayed properly by the browser, but it can be complicated for the developer to interpret the content.
